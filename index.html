Heart

The heart is a vital muscular organ responsible for pumping blood through the body's circulatory system, ensuring oxygen and nutrients reach every cell. It is located in the chest cavity, slightly to the left of the center. The heart operates continuously, beating around 100,000 times a day to sustain life.

Lungs

The lungs are essential organs of the respiratory system, responsible for exchanging oxygen and carbon dioxide between the blood and the air we breathe. Located in the chest on either side of the heart, they are protected by the ribcage. The lungs work tirelessly to support breathing, enabling oxygen to fuel the bodyâ€™s cells and removing waste gases.

Brain

The human brain is a complex organ that serves as the control center for the body, responsible for thoughts, emotions, memory, and coordinating bodily functions. It consists of billions of neurons and is divided into regions such as the cerebrum, cerebellum, and brainstem, each with specialized roles. Protected by the skull, the brain continuously processes sensory information and directs responses to sustain life and enable learning.

Kidney

The human kidneys are two bean-shaped organs located on either side of the spine, just below the ribcage, responsible for filtering waste and excess fluids from the blood to form urine. They help regulate blood pressure, electrolyte balance, and red blood cell production. Essential for maintaining the body's internal environment, the kidneys ensure the removal of toxins and sustain overall health.

Diaphragm

The human diaphragm is a dome-shaped muscle located beneath the lungs, playing a crucial role in breathing by contracting and relaxing to allow air exchange. It separates the thoracic cavity (chest) from the abdominal cavity and supports other functions like speech and coughing. The diaphragm also helps maintain pressure within the abdomen, aiding in digestion and organ stability.

Skeletal System

The human skeletal system provides the framework for the body, supporting structure, protecting vital organs, and enabling movement through joints and muscles. It consists of 206 bones in adulthood, along with cartilage, ligaments, and tendons. The skeletal system also plays a crucial role in producing blood cells and storing essential minerals like calcium and phosphorus.
